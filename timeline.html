January
Jan 7 – 31
Los Angeles Palisades Wildfire
Hurricane-force Santa Ana winds ignited devastating wildfires across LA County, destroying thousands of homes and forcing tens of thousands to evacuate. Insured losses were among the costliest in U.S. history.
Disaster
Jan 19
Gaza Ceasefire Takes Effect
After months of negotiations brokered by the U.S. and Qatar, Israel and Hamas agreed to a ceasefire, pausing a war that had killed tens of thousands since October 2023. The truce included hostage and prisoner exchanges.
Conflict
Jan 20
Trump Inaugurated as 47th President
Donald Trump was sworn in for a second non-consecutive term. He immediately began signing a historic wave of executive orders — 225 by year's end — targeting immigration, energy, and government operations.
Politics
Jan 27
DeepSeek Shakes Global Markets
China's DeepSeek released an AI chatbot rivaling ChatGPT, triggering a global tech selloff. Nvidia lost nearly $600 billion in a single day — the largest single-company market cap drop in U.S. stock history.
Science & Tech
Jan 29
Potomac River Mid-Air Collision
A U.S. Army Black Hawk helicopter collided with a PSA Airlines regional jet on approach to Reagan National Airport in Washington, D.C. All 67 people aboard both aircraft were killed in the deadliest U.S. air disaster in decades.

February
Feb
Tomb of Thutmose II Discovered
Egypt announced the discovery of the tomb of Pharaoh Thutmose II by a joint British-Egyptian team — the first royal tomb found since Tutankhamun's in 1922, and the last of the Eighteenth Dynasty pharaohs to be located.
Science
Feb 17
Indonesia Nationwide Protests
Mass protests erupted across Indonesia after legislation was enacted to increase military involvement in civilian government. The demonstrations became some of the largest in the country's recent history.
Politics
March
Mar 18
Israel Breaks Gaza Ceasefire
Israel launched a surprise military operation in Gaza, shattering the January ceasefire. The renewed offensive drew international condemnation and plunged the region back into full-scale conflict.
Conflict
Mar
Russia Reclaims Kursk Province
Russian forces recaptured the Kursk region, which Ukraine had seized in a surprise invasion in August 2024. The Ukraine war ground into its fourth year with Russia losing an estimated 1,000 soldiers per day.
Conflict
April
Apr 21
Pope Francis Dies
Pope Francis died suddenly after suffering a stroke, ending a papacy of twelve years. The Argentine-born pontiff was known for his progressive social stance, humility, and outreach beyond the Catholic faithful. The Church entered interregnum.
Culture
May
May
First American Pope Elected
Cardinal Robert Prevost of Chicago was elected as Pope Leo XIV — the first American to lead the Catholic Church in its 2,000-year history. Born on Chicago's South Side and a Villanova graduate, he had spent decades doing missionary work in Peru.
Culture
June
Jun 12 – 24
The Twelve-Day War: Israel vs. Iran
Years of tensions erupted into open conflict. Israel struck Iran's military commanders, nuclear scientists, and strategic targets, joined by U.S. bombers. A ceasefire brokered by the U.S. and Qatar ended the fighting on June 24.
Conflict
Jun
Ukraine's Operation Spiderweb
Ukraine stunned the world with a covert drone strike deep into Russia, hitting five air bases simultaneously. While it showcased Ukraine's growing capabilities, experts noted it did not shift the war's fundamental dynamics.
Conflict
Jun 14
Minnesota Lawmaker Assassinated
Minnesota State Rep. Melissa Hortman and her husband were shot and killed at their home in a politically motivated attack. State Sen. John Hoffman and his wife were also shot in a separate incident the same day.
Terror
July
Jul
"One Big Beautiful Bill" Signed
President Trump signed sweeping legislation authorizing troops at the southern border, cutting federal spending across major programs including SNAP, and establishing the "Golden Dome" aerial defense system.
Politics
September
Sep 7
Carlo Acutis Canonized
The teenage web developer Carlo Acutis, who died of leukemia at 15, was canonized by Pope Leo XIV — becoming known as "the first millennial saint" and "patron saint of the Internet" for his digital evangelism.
Culture
Sep 10
Charlie Kirk Assassinated
Conservative activist and Turning Point USA founder Charlie Kirk was shot and killed during a campus event at Utah Valley University in Utah. The assassination sent shockwaves through U.S. politics and intensified debates over political violence.
Terror
Sep 8
Nepal Gen Z Uprising
Thousands of students stormed government buildings in Kathmandu, protesting a sweeping social media ban. The uprising grew into one of the most dramatic Gen Z-led protests of the year, forcing the government into crisis.
Politics
October
Oct 10 – 13
Gaza Ceasefire & Hostages Released
A second ceasefire took effect in the Israel-Hamas war. Over the following days, the last 20 living Israeli hostages were returned. The conflict had lasted roughly two years since Hamas's October 2023 assault.
Conflict
Oct
Hurricane Melissa Hits Jamaica
Hurricane Melissa became the strongest storm to ever strike Jamaica, making landfall as a Category 5 and causing widespread devastation. It capped a year of catastrophic climate events with over $105B in global insured losses.
Disaster
November
Nov
U.S. Government Shutdown Begins
Political gridlock led to the longest government shutdown in years, beginning just before Thanksgiving. The crisis stemmed from deep disagreements over healthcare, defense spending, and the fallout of Trump's sweeping domestic agenda.
Politics
Nov 5
Democrats Sweep 2025 Elections
In off-year elections seen as an early referendum on the Trump presidency, Democrats swept nearly every competitive race across the country, reportedly rattling Republican strategists heading into 2026 midterms.
Politics
December
Dec 6
Messi Wins MLS Cup with Inter Miami
Lionel Messi led Inter Miami CF to its first-ever MLS Cup championship, capping a fairy-tale chapter in the Argentine legend's career and electrifying American soccer audiences.
Culture
Dec 10
Australia Bans Social Media for Under-16s
Australia implemented the world's strictest social media laws, banning access for anyone under 16 across platforms including YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and X. The move inspired similar legislation globally.
Politics
Dec 14
Bondi Beach Shooting
Two gunmen opened fire on a Hanukkah celebration at Bondi Beach in Sydney, killing 15 people and injuring over 40 in what became the deadliest terrorist attack in Australian history. One gunman was killed by police.
